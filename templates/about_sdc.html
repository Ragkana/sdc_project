Background

With the Asia-Pacific being the most hazard and disaster-prone region in the world to natural hazards and the increasing impacts of climate change, Switzerland in its International Cooperation Strategy 2021-2024 stipulates an increased commitment for Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) and Climate Change Adaptation (CCA).

Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation (SDC) Regional Advisory Hub for Southeast Asia and the Pacific, in Bangkok is designed to advise on disaster risk management measures, including nature-based solutions, while contributing to climate change adaptation and supporting sustainable management of natural resources in an effective and targeted manner. GIS applications and spatial analysis can also be useful for capacity development and operational decision-making. It will also be able to enhance its support for mainstreaming DRR and disaster risk management into SDC projects in the region, namely in Laos and Cambodia by customizing hazard and multi hazard maps and identifying if SDC projects are at risk of disasters.

In this context, a project was implemented by the Regional Integrated Multi-Hazard Early Warning System (RIMES) with funding from SDC through its Regional Advisory Hub for Southeast Asia and the Pacific, Bangkok (or the SDC Regional Hub, Bangkok) to collate and compile existing data on disasters, hydromet time series data and present it via a dedicated portal. This portal would serve as an aid in decision-making needs of the SDC Regional Advisory Hub in Bangkok.

Objective of the assignment

The project aims to support SDC Regional Hub, Bangkok in its roles and responsibilities across regional, national and thematic pillars through its work streams on monitoring, analysing, reporting and advising on hazard events and disasters for rapid response, mainstreaming DRR into SDC programmes/ projects/ specific interventions in the region, and cooperation with regional intergovernmental organisations.

In the context of disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation, risk assessments and hazard mapping that are relevant to operational decision-making become vital. Variables contributing to risk at a location can be broadly categorized as static (or quasi-static) (such as topography, land use, soil population, socio-economic) and dynamic (such as weather, crop-status, hydrology, hazards).

Through SDC-RIMES collaboration a restricted-access web-based tool has been developed to provide guidance for dynamic risk assessment in a phased manner focused on Cambodia and Lao PDR. This is expected to be developed and rolled-out in subsequent phases to encompass additional features, aspects and include Myanmar.

The overall objective of the engagement is to create a restricted access web-platform as a decision support tool for SDC Regional Hub-Bangkok, incorporating various readily available datasets on disaster risks, hazards, vulnerabilities and exposure along with forecast data. Specifically, RIMES would:

  • Collect data in geospatial data format and generate an integrated display of hydro-met parameters based on past flooding / storms / other severe weather events to indicative alerts in combination with forecasts.
  • Create a restricted access web-platform as a decision support tool for SDC Regional Advisory Hub, incorporating various readily available datasets on disaster risks, hazards, vulnerabilities and exposure along with forecast data.

Data Collection

The assignment commenced in earnest upon signing of the contract agreement between SDC and RIMES on 18 January 2022, with focus on collection and collation of data related to Cambodia and Lao PDR on the following:

  1. past disasters- to map past and current hydrological and meteorological hazards based on past occurrences
  2. relevant socio-economic data sets (population, GDP) that are readily available
  3. administrative maps up to provincial and commune/ village level that are readily available.
